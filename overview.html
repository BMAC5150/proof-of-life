Overview

PROOF OF LIFE is a high-stakes geopolitical techno-thriller about identity, institutional failure, and the terrifying fragility of a world where existence itself depends on what the system says is true. At once intimate and explosive, it is the story of brilliant people on every side of a gathering catastrophe, and of the moment when seeing the danger is no longer enough.

A small startup in lower Manhattan builds software that does one simple thing: it cleans up the mess inside community banks where the same customer exists as three different people in three different systems. The product works. It works so well that every connected institution treats it as the final word on who their customers are. Then a late-night coding error erases tens of thousands of people from the financial systems that prove they exist, and the four young founders discover that their product has quietly evolved into something else entirely: a tool that can make anyone disappear from the institutional infrastructure that sustains modern life. Their bank cards stop working. Their prescriptions cannot be filled. Their mortgages lose their names. They become, in every way that matters to the systems that run society, no one. Before the founders can decide what to do with what they have built, the architecture is stolen through a chain that connects a Venezuelan cartel investor, a retired United States Senator, and a venture capital firm on Broadway to a bunker outside Tehran, where young Iranian cyber operators under threat of American bombing are constructing the most consequential weapon their country has ever possessed. It is not a bomb. It is the ability to hold a city hostage by erasing the people whose identities hold it together.

Four worlds collide: a Chinatown walkup where brilliant twentysomethings are trapped between survival and conscience; a bunker outside Tehran where an equally brilliant young operator begins to understand what he is building; the fourteenth floor of a Manhattan venture capital firm where a twenty-seven-year-old analyst carries knowledge her institution has no mechanism to hear; and a desk at Fort Meade where a Navy commander on his final posting watches a shape form in a stack of paper nobody else in the building is looking at. In a world where identity has become digital, access has become permission, and permission can be revoked by systems no one fully understands, Proof of Life asks the question that should keep everyone awake: what happens when the systems that know who you are decide you were never there?

Proof of Life is a geopolitical thriller in the tradition of Tom Clancy and John le Carré, with the contemporary urgency of a world that has made human identity dependent on infrastructure it cannot see, does not control, and has never stress-tested against the people smart enough to break it.
